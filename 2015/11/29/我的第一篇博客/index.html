    Abstract：This study presents a new reference current estimation method using proposed robust extended complex Kalman filter
    (RECKF) together with model predictive current (MPC) control strategy in the development of a three-phase shunt active power
    filter (SAPF). A new exponential function embedded into the RECKF algorithm helps in the estimation of in phase fundamental
    component of voltage (vh) at the point of common coupling considering grid perturbations such as distorted voltage, measurement
    noise and phase angle jump and also for the estimation of fundamental amplitude of the load current (ih). The estimation of these
    two variables (vh, ih) is used to generate reference signals for MPC. The proposed RECKF-MPC needs less number of voltage
    sensors and resolves the difficulty of gain tuning of proportional–integral (PI) controller. The proposed RECKF-MPC approach is
    implemented using MATLAB/SIMULINK and also Opal-RT was used to obtain the real-time results. The results obtained using
    the proposed RECKF together with different variants of Kalman filters (Kalman filter (KF), extended KF (EKF) and extended
    complex KF (ECKF)) and PI controller are analysed both in the steady state as well as transient state conditions. From the
    above experimentation, it was observed that the proposed RECKF-MPC control strategy outperforms over PI controller and
    other variants of Kalman filtering approaches in terms of reference tracking error, power factor distortion and percentage total
    harmonic distortion in the SAPF system.

1 Introduction

Shunt active power filters (SAPFs) have become popular from
the research community over past 10 years. They have the
potential to offer controlled harmonic reduction and power
quality improvement on power systems. This gives them a
significant advantage when compared with other harmonic
correction techniques [1–5]. Passive shunt filters for
instance can introduce a resonance onto the power system
and they also have no current limit, therefore can become
overloaded if the supply voltage quality deteriorates.
To obtain efficient shunt active filter performance, the
source reference generation plays an important role because
of its simplicity and less time consuming in
implementation. External proportional–integral (PI)
controller loop is needed for regulation of dc bus voltage in
the method based on conventional unit vector [6]. Fryze
theory [7] requires both load current and source voltage
measurement for reference generation without the necessity
of PI controller loop. However, it needs more calculations
for implementation. The operation of the SAPF system
requires ac mains to supply real power needed to the load
and some losses (switching losses of devices, losses in the
reactor and dielectric losses of dc capacitor) in the SAPF.
Therefore the proposed reference source currents that are
used to decide the switching of the SAPF have two parts:
one is the real fundamental-frequency component of the
load current, which is estimated using the proposed robust extended complex Kalman filter (RECKF), and another
component, which corresponds to the losses in the SAPF, is
also estimated using our proposed RECKF over the dc
voltage of SAPF. The advantage of this method is the
simplicity of calculus, avoidance of tuning methods and
the reduced number of voltage sensors necessary to obtain
the current reference. The reason for using this approach
is to eliminate relatively large overshoots and undershoots
associated with conventional PI controls, which may disturb
the stable operation of the inverter in protecting the
switching devices. Furthermore, recent applications need
faster response, minimum power dissipation and robustness,
which the PI controller fails to satisfy. Thus it is expected
that our proposed reference generation can overcome the
demerits of PI control and makes the dc-link capacitor
voltage steady at about set point.

However, if the fundamental component is not estimated
accurately, the active filter will source or sink the active
power. Among the several estimation methods [8–14], both
linear and extended Kalman filtering approaches have
attracted widespread attention, as they accurately estimate
the amplitude, phase and frequency of a signal buried with
noise and harmonics. Owing to the dynamic nature of
power system, the estimation method chosen must be
capable of rejecting grid perturbations such as harmonics,
voltage distortion, phase angle jump and measurement
noise. The aforesaid estimation approaches have not
considered these grid perturbations and hence in view of addressing these above issues (harmonics, voltage distortion,
noise), we focus on the development of a new estimation
algorithm (RECKF), in which a weighted exponential
function e−(y−h(x))2
has been incorporated considering all the
above grid perturbations to estimate the reference current in
SAPF. With arising abnormal condition, the value of ‘y − h
(x)’ increases faster because of inclusion of a ‘square’ term
in the proposed function, as a result the influences of
abnormality can be decreased at a faster rate as compared
with the exponential function mentioned earlier in [15, 16]
and hence the proposed RECKF approach provides
improved estimation which is independent of all grid
perturbations.

Successful control of active filters requires an accurate
current control scheme in addition with reference
generation. Implementation of new and more complex
control techniques, for example, sliding mode, fuzzy,
adaptive and predictive control have been possible with the
development of powerful and fast microprocessors.
Particularly, model predictive current (MPC) [17–20] has
demonstrated to offer a very simple and effective alternative
to classical control algorithms with pulse width modulation
for controlling the gate signals. The MPC approach takes
advantage of the limited number of switching states of
voltage source inverter (VSI) for solving optimisation
problem. A discrete model is used to predict the behaviour
of the system for every admissible actuation sequence up to
the prediction horizon. The switching action that minimises
a predefined cost function is finally selected to be applied in the next sampling instant. The main advantage of MPC lies
in the direct application of the control action to the inverter,without requiring a modulation stage.

With aforementioned views our proposed RECKF-MPC
approach is expected to be a better control strategy in SAPF
system. This paper is organised as follows. In Section 2, the
mathematical model of the SAPF is presented, followed by
the explanation of the current controller scheme and
proposed reference generation algorithms in Sections 3 and
4, respectively. A brief description of Opal-RT real-time
platform is mentioned in Section 5. To assess the
performance of the proposed control scheme, extensive
simulation results are presented in Section 6 and verified by
real-time Opal-RT Lab. Finally, the conclusion is
summarised in Section 7.

5 Real-time OPAL-RT platform
The RT-LAB simulator [21, 22] enables the parallel simulation of
power drives and electric circuits on clusters of PC running quick
UNIX (QNX) or RT-Linux operating systems. Using standard
SIMULINK models, RT–LAB builds computation and
communication tasks necessary to make parallel simulation of
electrical systems with standard PCs and communication links.
The Opal-RT allows developers to prove their ideas, prototypes
and final products in a realistic environment. It is a fully digital
power system simulator performing electromagnetic transient
power system simulations with a typical time step of 50 µs
with a combination of custom software and hardware. It is an
ideal tool for the design, development and testing of power
system protection and control schemes.
Fig. 2a shows the real-time simulation machine and its
console monitor. The results are observed in a digital
storage oscilloscope, which is connected to RT-Lab
simulator via connecting probes. The OP5142 displayed in
Fig. 2b is one of the key building blocks in the modulator
OP5000 I/O system from Opal-RT Technologies. The
incorporation of field programmable gate array (FPGA)
technologies in RT-Lab simulation clusters are allowed for
distributed execution of hardware description language
functions and high-speed digital I/O in real-time models. S1
is connected to master reset signal of OP5142 board and
this sends a reset signal to all OP5142 subsystems. JTAG1
gives access to OP5142 joint test action group (JTAG)
chain and its use is to configure the flash memory with its
default configuration file. Depending on the ‘JUMP4’
jumper presence, this interface may give access to both
FPGA and complex programmable logic device (CPLD)
configurations. If ‘JUMP4’ jumpers are set to independent
mode, JTAG2 gives access to CPLD JTAG configuration
interface. JTAG connection enables users to programme
manually the programmable components on the board.
JTAG3 gives access to the peripheral component interconnect express bridge JTAG interface. JP1 implements
all data and power transfers needed to be done with
external load. J1, J2 and J3 exchange all I/O-related data to
I/O module, including identification data, serial

From the steady-state response, our proposed RECKF is
found to be the best among the four KF [23, 24]
algorithms, but slightly better than conventional PI
controller. To further assess the performance of our proposed RECKF with conventional PI controller, the
transient test analyses were performed in the SAPF system.
Figs. 7 and 8 show the transient responses of load current,

compensating current is smoother in case of RECKF as
compared with PI controller as given in Fig. 7c. As shown
in Fig. 7d, although the source current waveforms of both
RECKF and PI controller are always sinusoidal even when
subjected to a changing non-linear load, the source current
waveforms of the RECKF are fine and smoother than that
of the PI. The results obtained from the real-time simulator,
Opal-RT are shown in (Fig. 8), which are in close

Fig. 9 shows the comparison of the compensation effects
for both the PI-MPC and RECKF-MPC-based SAPF system
in steady state as well as transient states. For PI-MPC-based
SAPF, the source current THD is reduced from 24.9%
(without SAPF) to 4.65% (steady state) and 5.32%
(transient state). The PF is improved from 0.65 (without
SAPF) to 0.99 (steady state) and 0.98 (transient state).
However, the system performance becomes worst if load
changes. On the other hand, the same tests were performed,
except that the proposed RECKF approach is used instead
of the PI one. The source current THD is dropped to about
4.46% (steady state) and 4.67% (transient state) in case of
RECKF. PI control gains are tuned for steady-state
condition to obtain the best response. When the transient

state occurs, the gains of the PI controller cannot ensure
that the system has same response because of the lack of
adaptability. However, in RECKF-MPC-based SAPF
systems, a considerable flexibility is achieved to the
designer to alter the goals by quickly modifying the
weighted exponential function under abnormal condition
(i.e. change from steady to transient condition). Hence,
RECKF-MPC approach can assure the system stability.

7 Conclusions
In this paper, a model predictive-based SAPF with a new
reference current estimation scheme has been presented.
This scheme exploits the estimation of in phase fundamental
component of distorted PCC voltage along with the
estimation of fundamental amplitude of load current using
KF, EKF, ECKF and proposed RECKF algorithms. The
proposed RECKF algorithm is based on applying a new
weighted exponential function as a factor to limit the
variation of innovation vector, to restrain the unusual
measured value and to enhance the estimated accuracy with
consideration of grid perturbations such as voltage
distortion, measurement noise and phase angle jump. MPC
strategy presented in this paper is very simple and powerful
and advantageously considers the discrete nature of power converters. In addition, it is not necessary to include any
type of modulator and the drive signals for the IGBTs are
generated directly by this control. The proposed
RECKF-MPC control strategy avoids the use of external
linear and non-linear controllers; hence a cheaper control
strategy can be implemented while high performance is
maintained. The performances of the proposed
RECKF-MPC-based SAPF have been verified both in
steady state and transient state conditions. The proposed
RECKF approach overcomes difficulties encountered with
the fixed-gain PI controller, such as flexibility and
robustness over stabilisation of capacitor voltage when
changing loads.
Determination of current reference and current controller
for SAPF is one of the most important issues in
improvement of power quality. From the real-time and
simulation results, it is observed that RECKF-MPC exhibits
excellent tracking performance thus is a better control
approach to SAPF design in steady state as well as transient
state condition which improves power quality more
effectively in terms of efficient harmonics mitigation, power
factor improvement and tracking error reduction in presence
of above all grid perturbations.
